About

In the middle of the Iraq War, T. Miller got an email file from a source who said a story of corruption, rape and murder was buried within. But he vanished, leaving Miller with more than 15,000 emails and no idea of where to look. The story never got done. It was one of those painful moments in every reporter’s life: the big one that got away.

Five years later, Miller, an experienced journalist, connected with Paul and Matt Quinn, experienced coders. They decided to work together to solve this problem. Minezy is the answer – in alpha.

As a prototype, Minezy examines only MS Outlook files. For purposes of demonstration, we have loaded up the Enron Corpus – some 500,000 emails released by a judge after the end of the Enron case. The current version allows you to explore Enron’s deepest secrets – every email sent and received over a two year period.

We’d love for you to play with it. See if you can find anything out. Does the design work? The analysis? Is it a tool that would benefit reporters?

It’s just the start, we hope. As we raise funds and develop the prototype, our first aim is to make Minezy capable of accepting reporters’ own email dumps, whether from a source or from a Freedom of Information Act request. We want to be able to accept multiple formats. We want to create a rock solid secure platform, available as a download install or a cloud version. And we want to build in an OCR engine for those poor, poor souls who get 300,000 paper print-outs from some lame state or federal agency bent on keeping their secret stuff secret.

None of this will be easy. But with your help, it will be worthful. It will be fun. And it will be an important tool to allow reporters to mine the small data of an email file for the relationships, dates and content faster, better and more accurately than before.

In short, we want to make sure there are no more T. Miller’s who let potentially big stories slip from their grasp because there’s just too much data to mine. We want to make sure the big story doesn’t get away. We want to make sure you get it, and spread it to the world.
